Users possess great skill and knowledge in using and wielding Ofuda, a talisman made out of various materials such as paper, wood, cloth or metal. Ofuda are commonly found in both Shinto shrines and Buddhist temples and are considered to be imbued with the power of the gods and in Daoist traditions they are called Fulu, which is also a term for Daoist incantations and magic symbols, written or painted as talisman or Lingfu by Daoist practitioners. The users are also able to wield them with great proficiency and skill for mystical and spiritual purposes that can be used to their advantages. They can also be used to dispel dark energy.
