User with this ability either is or can transform into an Ahuizotl (Nahuatl Ähuitzotl for "spiny aquatic thing", a.k.a. "water dog") a legendary creature of Aztec mythology commonly described as water dog-like being with waterproof fur that clumps up to create spikes (hence its name). The ahuizotl has hands capable of manipulation and an additional hand on its tail. The ahuizotl is feared due to its liking for human flesh, especially nails, eyes, and teeth. It is said to live in or near the water and to use the hand on the end of its tail to snatch its prey, dragging the person into the depths to drown them.
