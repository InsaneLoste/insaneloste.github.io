The user can create, manipulate and otherwise shape chitin, a long-chain polymer of a N-acetylglucosamine, a derivative of glucose, found in many places throughout the natural world. In its pure, unmodified form, chitin is translucent, pliable, resilient, and quite tough. It is the main component of the cell walls of fungi, the exoskeletons of arthropods such as crustaceans and insects, the radulae of molluscs, and the beaks and internal shells of cephalopods.
