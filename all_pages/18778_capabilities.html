The user can create, shape and manipulate the philosopher's stone, a specialized substance said to be capable of bringing about both physical and metaphysical transformations in whatever subjects to which it is exposed. Considered the most well-known and desirable material in alchemy, it is said to take various forms: a stone, a powder, a tincture, even as a liquid (in this form, it is sometimes called the elixir of life). Creating the stone requires the usage of prima materia (first matter), the primordial base form of all matter. Through a secret, specialized process known as the Great Work, involving the four steps of nigredo (blackening or decomposition), albedo (whiteness or purification), citrinitas (yellowness or yellow dawn/awakening) and rubedo (redness or alchemical process; the end of the material's manifestation), one was able to bring the stone into existence.
