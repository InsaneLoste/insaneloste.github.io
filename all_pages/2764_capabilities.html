The user can create, shape, and manipulate bio-generated mechanochemical reaction chemistry, the practice of controlling the relationships between chemical reactions and mechanical operations. It can range from something as simple as rubbing your middle finger and thumb together in order to create noise to striking a match against a rough surface in order to spark a fire. Both of which are largely applicable through the use of mechanical force to induce chemical reactions, i.e., water (electricity) passing through hydraulic turbines (rotational) using currents to churn out power.
