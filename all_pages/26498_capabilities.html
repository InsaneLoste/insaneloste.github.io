The user can manipulate uranium, a silvery-white, weakly radioactive metal. It is malleable, ductile, slightly paramagnetic, strongly electropositive, and a poor electrical conductor. Uranium reacts with almost all non-metal elements (except for the noble gases) and their compounds, increasing reactivity with temperature. When finely divided, it can react with cold water; uranium metal becomes coated with a dark layer of uranium oxide in the air. It also exists in bones and liver.
