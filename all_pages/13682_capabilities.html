The user can manipulate kundalini, a form of divine, feminine life-force that lies dormant at the base of the spine. The word comes from Sanskrit, bearing the meaning “coiled snake;” this is is a reference to its traditional representation as a serpent of pure power. In the tradition of the chakras, it inhabits the Muladhara/Root Chakra and is the basis for Kundalini yoga. Through practices like meditation, breathing and mantras, one is capable of awakening the Kundalini, allowing access to this supernatural, primordial essence. It can travel through the other chakras and can grant one various abilities ranging from psychic powers like clairaudience/clairvoyance to physical/spiritual transcendence.
